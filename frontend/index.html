How Macro Monitor Works

Dollar Monitor

Inputs

  • DXY — Trade-Weighted U.S. Dollar Index (DTWEXBGS)
  • 10Y Yield — 10-Year Treasury Constant Maturity Rate
  • Breakeven — 10-Year Breakeven Inflation Rate
  • S&P 500 — S&P 500 Index

USD Score

A weighted composite of four normalized trend signals: DXY trend direction (40%), real yield trend (30%), inverted gold trend (20%), and inverted S&P 500 trend (10%). Trends are computed as the percentage difference between 20-day and 100-day moving averages, clamped to [-5%, 5%] and normalized to [-1, 1]. Real yield uses z-score normalization.

A score above +0.20 is STRONG USD. Below −0.20 is WEAK USD. In between is NEUTRAL.

USD Regimes

  • REAL RATES — DXY trending up with rising real yields. Dollar strength from rate differentials.
  • RISK-OFF — DXY trending up while stocks fall. Classic flight-to-safety.
  • FALLING REAL RATES — DXY trending down with declining real yields. Rate differential compressing.
  • HARD ASSETS — DXY trending down while gold trends up. Capital rotating to hard assets.
  • MIXED — No dominant pattern. Signals conflicting.

Gold Monitor

Inputs

  • Gold — London Gold Fixing Price (GOLDAMGBD228NLBM)
  • Plus shared inputs: 10Y Yield, Breakeven, S&P 500, DXY

Gold Score

A weighted composite: gold trend (40%), inverted real yield trend (30%, falling real yields = bullish gold), breakeven trend (20%, rising inflation expectations = bullish gold), and inverted DXY trend (10%, weak dollar = tailwind).

A score above +0.20 is STRONG GOLD. Below −0.20 is WEAK GOLD. In between is NEUTRAL.

Gold Regimes

  • REAL RATES — Gold trending up while real yields fall. Classic gold bull case.
  • INFLATION — Gold trending up with rising breakeven inflation. Inflation hedge bid.
  • RISK-OFF — Gold up while stocks and dollar both fall. Fear-driven safe haven.
  • RISING REAL RATES — Gold trending down as real yields rise. Opportunity cost headwind.
  • STRONG DOLLAR — Gold trending down while DXY trends up. Dollar headwind.
  • MIXED — No dominant pattern.

Reading the Relationship

The cross-monitor macro regime is derived from the combination of the USD and Gold signals. Neither monitor tells the full story alone — the relationship between them reveals the macro environment.

  • RISK ON / RATES RISING — Strong dollar + weak gold. Classic tightening: rates up, dollar up, gold suppressed.
  • REFLATION / STAGFLATION — Weak dollar + strong gold. Dollar weakening as gold confirms inflation or stress.
  • STRESS / SAFE HAVEN — Both strong. Unusual — both acting as safe havens. Fear signal.
  • DISINFLATIONARY RISK ON — Both weak. Risk assets leading; neither dollar nor gold bid.
  • GOLD-SPECIFIC DRIVER — Neutral dollar + strong gold. Gold moving on idiosyncratic factors.
  • USD TECHNICAL MOVE — Strong dollar + neutral gold. Dollar strength without gold confirmation.
  • MIXED — No clear combined read.

Interpretation

Each monitor and the relationship bar includes a one-sentence interpretation generated by Claude (Anthropic's AI), which receives all raw and derived data and produces a concise explanation of the current macro dynamics.
